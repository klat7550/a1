Professor Florence Nightingale

Florence Nightingale was an English social reformer and statistician, and the founder of modern nursing. She came to prominence while serving as a manager of nurses during the Crimean War, in which she organized care for wounded soldiers. Nightingale gave nursing a favourable reputation and became and icon of Victorian culture.
